Motivation

Programming is a rich medium for creative expression and problem solving for young learners. But managing student-directed creative work in the K-12 classroom can be challenging, particularly when students pursue their own projects and get stuck in a variety of ways.

Having opportunities to name and practice strategies for "getting unstuck" can build students' capacities for debugging and troubleshooting—potentially making it easier for teachers to include open-ended creative work in classroom activities.

Background and Design

Scratch is a graphical programming language and online community being used by millions of young people around the world. Inspired both by the strategies of young Scratch creators and a review of the debugging literature, we used an iterative, design-based research approach to create a collection of activities for teachers and students.

About Us

Getting Unstuck is developed by the Creative Computing Lab at the Harvard Graduate School of Education. We are a team of researchers, artists, educators, and designers excited about supporting teachers and learners in their creative pursuits.
